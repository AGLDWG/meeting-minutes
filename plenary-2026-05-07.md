### 4. Presentation

**Prof Eero Hyvönen**: Digital Humanities on the Semantic Web: from Infrastructure to Practical Applications, AI-based Knowledge Discovery, and Web of Wisdom

**Abstract**: Publishing Cultural Heritage (CH) data and Digital Humanities (DH) has evolved in phases from text carving and handwriting to printing texts, to publishing texts on-line, to publishing FAIR linked data comprehensible to computers for data enrichment and analysis, and to AI-based knowledge discovery on the Web, a "Web of Wisdom". In this presentation, lessons learned are overviewed on developing a national Semantic Web infrastructure [2] in Finland and over 20 in-use systems based on it, with up to millions of end users during 2002-2026 [3].  The underlying linked open data CH cloud [5] in these applications include knowledge graphs for museum collections, bibliographical data in libraries, medieval and renaissance manuscripts, military history, biographies, narratives, historical letter data, archaeological finds, legislation, parliamentary speeches, and art. Targeted to Digital Humanities researchers, application developers, and the public, these systems contain a Linked Open Data (LOD) service with a live SPARQL endpoint that can be used with modest programming skills for DH research, and a ready-to-use semantic portal on top of it. This work has led into the so-called Sampo Model for creating and publishing CH Knowledge Graphs (KG) as LOD services and semantic portal User Interfaces (UI).

**Bio**: [Eero Hyvönen](https://seco.cs.aalto.fi/u/eahyvone/) is professor (emer.) of semantic media technology at the Aalto University, Department of Computer Science, director (emer.) of the Helsinki Centre for Digital Humanities (HELDIG) at the University of Helsinki, and Research Affiliate at the Geneva Graduate Institute, Switzerland. His research has focused since 2001 on Semantic Web and Linked (Open) Data technologies, developing with his [Semantic Computing Research Group (SeCo)](https://seco.cs.aalto.fi/) the [national semantic web infrastructure in Finland](https://seco.cs.aalto.fi/projects/fin-clariah/) and its applications  (<https://seco.cs.aalto.fi/applications/sampo/>) as part of the pan-European DARIAH-EU infrastructure. The focus has been especially on Cultural Heritage and for Digital Humanities research. Eero Hyvönen has published some 600 articles and books and got some 30 international and national honorary prizes and acknowledgements.
